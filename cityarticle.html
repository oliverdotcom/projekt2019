Green Cities
of the Future

What are they, and why should you care?

Everybody talks about it, nobody really understands it: Green Cities.
By the end of this article you will be able to impress even the most ambitious tree hugger.

BY OLIVER JÜTZLER
25. OCTOBER 2019

What & why?

Well, as any sane person would, you could just look up the definition on Wikipedia. Fear not, we’ve already got it covered, but prepare yourself, it’s long and boring, and if you want you can just skip to the next paragraph: “A green/sustainable city is a city designed with consideration for social, economic, environmental impact and resilient habitat for existing populations, without compromising the ability of future generations to experience the same.”

While this sounds nice and sciency, you and I both know, that neither of us really understands what this means in practice. The thing is though, sustainability is on everyones lips these days. So whether you’re genuinely interested in learning about green cities, or just want to impress that hippie chick you like, you’ve come to the right place.



Colorful trashcans and silent busses

Green cities and living sustainably aren’t exactly new concepts. People have always lived in harmony with nature, and even though the industrial revolution might have put a dent in that concept, we’ve still been aware of sustainability, at least to some degree. But during the last couple of years, you might have noticed some changes around your city. Colorful trashcans appearing everywhere, telling you where to put what. Big, green busses that somehow don’t make any noise. Bikepaths taking up more space than the road. It’s almost 2020, and instead of flying cars and lightsabers, we’ve gotten rainwater tanks and organic vegetables. Where did we go wrong?
